Registration

How to register to vote in Houston

In Texas, voter registration is county-based. For Houston-area voters in Harris County, the safest approach is to use official state and county resources.

1. Confirm you are eligible

Texas says you must be a U.S. citizen, live in the county where you register, and be at least 17 years and 10 months old when you submit the application and 18 by Election Day.

Read eligibility rules

2. Submit the registration form

Texas requires a signed registration application. You can fill out the official form online, print it, sign it, and mail it, or request a printed application.

Start the official application

3. Track your status

After you apply, check your registration through the Secretary of State voter portal and look for your voter registration certificate in the mail.

Open My Voter Portal
Important: Texas generally requires registration at least 30 days before Election Day. Because mail timing can matter, this site should encourage users to submit forms as early as possible.

Districts

How to explain districts without misleading people

Congressional, legislative, judicial, and school board districts are based on a voter's full residential address, not just their ZIP code. A good Houston voter site should say that clearly.

Use ZIP for orientation

Helpful for neighborhood-level context, nearby election offices, and which school systems or cities a voter might be in.

Use the voter portal for exact districts

The official Texas voter portal is the best source for exact district and registration details tied to a person's address.

Use the sample ballot for real contests

The Harris County ballot lookup shows what is actually on a voter's ballot when an election is active.

Public School Governance

School board races matter, but superintendent races usually do not exist on the ballot

For most Texas public school systems, voters elect board members or trustees, and the board hires the superintendent. That means your site should usually track school board and trustee contests, not "superintendent candidates."

Houston ISD requires a special note

HISD is under a Texas Education Agency intervention. Official HISD and TEA pages say elected trustees had their governance responsibilities suspended in 2023, and TEA announced in June 2025 that the appointed board of managers would continue through June 1, 2027.
